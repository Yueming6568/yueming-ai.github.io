About Me

I am a tenure-track assistant professor at PRLab (模式识别实验室), School of Intelligence Science and Technology, Nanjing University (Suzhou Campus), directed by Professors Tieniu Tan (谭铁牛) and Caifeng Shan (单彩峰). I earned my PhD from CASIA in 2024, advised by Professor Tieniu Tan (谭铁牛) and co-advised by Professor Jing Dong (董晶). In 2024, I was a visiting researcher at the National University of Singapore.

My research focuses on generative models, large models and agents, and the foundational algorithms of embodied intelligence, together with complementary studies on safety issues that arise when these models are deployed in real-world systems.

In my leisure time, I like playing the guzheng, table tennis, hiking and travelling.

Research Directions

Content Generation & Editing

I study the architectural design and performance optimization of generative models and world models, and further investigate deployment-related safety issues such as model memorization leakage, privacy protection, and forged-content detection.

Large Models & Agents

I focus on foundation large models, RAG, agent architectures, inference acceleration, and multi-agent collaborative decision-making algorithms, while studying model safety alignment, adversarial attack and defense, and safety evaluation.

Embodied Intelligence

I study perception and decision-making algorithms for embodied intelligence, together with related safety challenges in embodied systems and their deployment scenarios.

AI Safety & Security

I work on building trustworthy AI systems for making network information credible, including misinformation detection, content provenance, robustness assessment, and security mechanisms for generative and agentic models, as part of NICE-PRLab @ NJU.
